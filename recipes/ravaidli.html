Rava Idli

Main Ingredients

  • For the Idli Batter:
    • Rava (semolina / suji) : 1 cup
    • Yogurt (curd) : 1 cup (slightly sour preferred)
    • Water : as needed to make thick batter
    • Eno fruit salt or baking soda : ½ tsp
    • Salt : to taste
  • For Tempering (Tadka):
    • Oil or ghee : 1 tbsp
    • Mustard seeds : ½ tsp
    • Urad dal : 1 tsp
    • Chana dal : 1 tsp
    • Cashews : 8 to 10 (optional)
    • Green chilies : 1 or 2 (finely chopped)
    • Ginger : 1 tsp (grated)
    • Curry leaves : 6 to 8
  • Optional Add-ins:
    • Grated carrot : 2 tbsp
    • Coriander leaves : 1 tbsp (chopped)
  • For Greasing:
    • Oil or ghee : for greasing idli molds

Steps to Prepare Rava Idli

  1. Heat 1 tbsp oil or ghee in a pan.
  2. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add urad dal, chana dal, and cashews; fry until golden.
  4. Add chopped green chilies, ginger, and curry leaves.
  5. Add rava and roast on medium heat for 5-6 minutes, until it smells nutty. Don’t brown it.
  6. Let the roasted rava cool slightly.
  7. In a bowl, mix roasted rava with curd and salt.
  8. Add water slowly to make a thick, pourable batter (like idli batter).
  9. Add grated carrot and coriander leaves if using.
  10. Let the batter rest for 10-15 minutes.
  11. Grease the idli molds with oil or ghee.
  12. Just before steaming, add Eno (or baking soda) and mix gently. Don't overmix.
  13. Immediately pour the batter into idli molds.
  14. Steam for 10-12 minutes or until a toothpick comes out clean.
  15. Let it rest for 2-3 minutes before removing from molds.
  16. Serve hot with coconut chutney or sambar.
